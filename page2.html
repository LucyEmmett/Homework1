Welcome to the Purple Page

Of all the shades of purple, lavender is that which is most associated with lesbians and the LGBTQ community as a whole.

An example of this is “The Lavender Song” (“Das Lila Lied”), an anthem of pride that emerged in 1920s Berlin.

There’s also the alleged lesbian dress codes of the 1920s, where “sprigs of violets” signalled a woman’s love for other women.

Gay men were referred to as “Lavender boys” in the 1920s as they were seen to be “not masculine enough.”
Surprisingly in 1926, a biography of Abraham Lincoln described him as possessing “a streak of lavender”, implying homosexual tendencies (or perhaps bisexuality in a modern reading).

In 1970, the Lavender Menace lesbian activist group stormed the stage of the Second Congress to Unite Women wearing t-shirts that said "Lavender Menace" ; they were responding to famous feminist Betty Friedan’s comment that lesbians were a “lavender menace” that would undermine the women’s movement!
